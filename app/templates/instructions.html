Overview


Most road traffic accidents happen when a driver fails to detect a critical event, like another car turning into their path. This is due to a neurological limitation common to all people: we have a finite amount of perceptual processing power, meaning that in a complicated scene we are able to process only a fraction of the available information.

The aim of this study is to discover what makes some driving situations more demanding than others, in other words: what about a situation makes detecting critical events more difficult?



Your Task


We'll be asking what kind of driving situations you find demanding. You'll be presented with two short video clips, around two seconds long, taken from a driver's point-of-view driving in a city. We want you to watch both videos and select which situation you'd find more demanding as a driver, Video 1 or Video 2, by clicking the button under that video. To help with this please put yourself in the mindset of the driver as you watch the videos!

After you have selected a video, a new pair of videos will be presented for you to watch. Keep watching and voting for the most demanding video as long as you feel comfortable - if you start to feel fatigued and can't concentrate fully on the task, take a break and come back another time. The more selections you make, the more we can learn about the science of driving.

To get a feel for the task, do some example comparisons by pressing the button below. You can only select the more demanding video once you've watched both videos on the page. Once you feel comfortable with the task, continue onto the proper experiment.
